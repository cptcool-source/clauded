New Patients

Starting your child's care with us is simple. Here's everything you need to know.

Getting Started

Three steps to your first visit.

1

Pre-register online

Complete your new patient registration through our healow portal before your first visit. It takes about 10 minutes and saves time at the office.

2

Gather your documents

Bring your insurance card, photo ID, and any existing medical records or vaccination history. The more we know upfront, the better we can care for your child.

3

Come on in

Arrive 15 minutes early for your first visit. We'll take care of the rest — introduction to our team, a thorough exam, and a care plan built around your child.

Begin Pre-Registration

What to bring

  • Insurance card & photo IDBoth parents' or guardian's ID and the child's insurance card if applicable.
  • Vaccination recordsYour child's immunization history, especially if transferring from another practice.
  • Previous medical recordsRelevant history, previous diagnoses, or specialist reports if transferring care.
  • Medications & allergiesA list of any current medications, supplements, and known allergies.
  • Completed new patient formsAvailable through the healow patient portal — completing these in advance saves time at check-in.

Insurance & payment

We accept most major insurance plans. Please call our office at 941-266-5629 to verify that your specific plan is accepted before your appointment.

We also offer flexible self-pay options for families without insurance coverage — quality care shouldn't depend on your insurance status.

Your First Visit

What to expect.

Your first visit with Dr. Patel is as much about getting to know your family as it is about examining your child. We'll take a thorough medical history, answer every question you bring, and make sure you leave feeling confident about your child's care plan — not confused or rushed.

We encourage you to bring a list of questions. No question is too small. We'd rather spend the extra five minutes answering something that's been on your mind than have you leave with uncertainty. That's what a gold standard practice looks like.

If your child becomes sick between establishing care and your first well visit, same-day sick appointments are available when we have capacity. Call early in the morning for the best chance of being seen the same day.
